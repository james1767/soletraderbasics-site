# Free Business Bank Accounts for Sole Traders UK (2026)

## Quick answer

You don't need to pay for a business bank account. Several UK banks offer genuinely free accounts for sole traders — no monthly fee, no minimum balance, and no requirement to be a registered limited company.

Here are the accounts that are actually free, not just "free for 12 months then we charge you."

## Permanently free accounts

### Starling Bank Business
- **Monthly fee:** £0
- **UK payments:** Free (bank transfers, Direct Debits, standing orders)
- **Cash deposits:** Free up to £1,000/month at any Post Office (3% fee above that)
- **Card:** Mastercard debit, contactless
- **App rating:** 4.7/5 — highest-rated UK business banking app
- **Accounting integrations:** Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent via open banking

Starling is our top recommendation for most sole traders. It's genuinely free with no strings, the app is excellent, and it now includes Accounting Essentials — free invoicing, expense categorisation, and Making Tax Digital submissions built right in. The free Post Office cash deposits (up to £1,000/month) are a bonus that most digital banks don't offer.

### Mettle by NatWest
- **Monthly fee:** £0
- **UK payments:** Free
- **Cash deposits:** Not available (fully digital)
- **Card:** Visa debit
- **Accounting integrations:** FreeAgent included free (full version, normally £33/month excl. VAT)

Mettle's standout feature is the free FreeAgent subscription — that's nearly £400/year you're not spending on accounting software. If you don't already have an accounting tool, Mettle + FreeAgent is the most cost-effective combination available for sole traders. The trade-off: no cash deposit facilities at all.

### Tide (Free plan)
- **Monthly fee:** £0 (paid plans available from £12.49/month with extra features)
- **UK payments:** 5 free bank transfers per month (20p per transfer after that)
- **Cash deposits:** 0.99% (min £2.50) at Post Office
- **Card:** Visa debit
- **Accounting integrations:** Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent, Sage
- **Built-in invoicing:** Yes — create, send, and track invoices from the app

Tide is the most popular business banking platform in the UK by account numbers. The free plan is limited to 5 bank transfers per month and 3 invoices — sole traders with regular clients will likely need a paid plan. The broad accounting integrations (Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent, Sage) are the main advantage over other options.

### Monzo Business Lite
- **Monthly fee:** £0
- **UK payments:** Free
- **Cash deposits:** £1 per deposit at PayPoint locations
- **Card:** Mastercard debit
- **Accounting integrations:** None on Lite plan (Xero and FreeAgent available on Pro at £5/month)

Monzo Lite is basic — it's essentially a second Monzo account with a business label. No invoicing, no accounting integrations, no tax pots. Those features are on the Pro plan (£5/month). Best for existing Monzo users who want simple separation between personal and business money, but you'll likely want to upgrade or use separate [accounting software](/best/accounting-software-sole-trader/).

## "Free for X months" accounts (then they charge)

Some traditional banks offer free introductory periods:

- **Lloyds Business Account** — free for 12 months, then £7/month
- **HSBC Business Account** — free for 12 months, then £8/month (or free if you maintain a minimum balance)
- **Barclays Business Account** — free for 12 months, then £8/month

These can work if you specifically want a high-street bank (e.g. you need branch access for regular large cash deposits). But for most sole traders, the permanently free digital banks above offer better features, better apps, and — crucially — stay free.

## How to choose

| Situation | Best Free Account |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Most sole traders** | Starling — best app, truly free, includes invoicing and accounting essentials |
| **Want free accounting software** | Mettle — comes with FreeAgent at no cost |
| **Want broad accounting integrations** | Tide — connects to Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent, and Sage |
| **Already use Monzo** | Monzo Lite — simple separation, familiar app |
| **Need branch access / large cash deposits** | Lloyds or Barclays (free for 12 months, then paid) |

Don't let the choice slow you down. Pick one and open it today — they're all free, they all work, and switching later is straightforward if you change your mind.
