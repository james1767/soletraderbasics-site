# Tide vs Starling for Sole Traders: Which Is Better?

## Quick answer

Tide and Starling are the two most popular free business accounts for UK sole traders. Both now offer invoicing, accounting integrations, and expense tracking — but they take different approaches. Starling bundles its own Accounting Essentials (including MTD submissions) for free. Tide focuses on broad third-party integrations and more granular expense tools.

Here's a direct comparison to help you pick.

## Head-to-head

| Feature | Starling | Tide |
|---------|----------|------|
| **Monthly fee** | £0 | £0 (free plan) |
| **UK bank transfers** | Free (unlimited) | 5 free/month (20p each after) |
| **Cash deposits** | Free up to £1,000/mo at Post Office | 0.99% (min £2.50) at Post Office |
| **Card** | Mastercard debit | Visa debit |
| **Built-in invoicing** | ✅ (free) | ✅ (3 free/month on free plan) |
| **Built-in accounting** | ✅ (Accounting Essentials + MTD) | ❌ (third-party integrations) |
| **Accounting integrations** | Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent | Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent, Sage |
| **Tax pots / savings** | ✅ (Savings Spaces) | ❌ (no built-in savings) |
| **App rating (App Store)** | ⭐ 4.7/5 | ⭐ 4.4/5 |
| **Phone support** | ✅ (all plans) | ❌ (paid plans only) |
| **FSCS protected** | ✅ £120,000 (Starling Bank) | ✅ £120,000 (via ClearBank) |

## Where Starling wins

**The app is better.** Starling consistently rates as the best banking app in the UK — personal or business. Transactions appear instantly, the interface is clean, and everything is where you'd expect it. Tide's app is good, but it's busier and occasionally sluggish.

**Unlimited free transfers.** Starling doesn't limit how many bank transfers you make. Tide's free plan caps you at 5 per month — sole traders with a handful of regular clients will hit that almost immediately.

**Free cash deposits.** You can deposit cash at any Post Office for free (up to £1,000/month). Tide charges 0.99% (min £2.50). If you handle any amount of cash — even occasionally — Starling saves you money.

**Built-in accounting essentials.** Starling now includes its own accounting tools — expense categorisation, invoicing, and Making Tax Digital submissions — for free, built into the app. You may not need separate accounting software at all for simple businesses.

**Phone support on the free plan.** If something goes wrong, you can call Starling and speak to a person. Tide reserves phone support for paid plans (from £12.49/month). The free plan gets chat and email only.

**Savings Spaces.** Starling lets you create separate savings pots within your business account — useful for setting aside tax money, saving for equipment, or ringfencing project budgets. Tide has no equivalent.

**Simpler.** If you just want a bank account that works — money in, money out, connected to your [accounting software](/best/accounting-software-sole-trader/) — Starling does it with less clutter.

## Where Tide wins

**Broader accounting integrations.** Tide connects to Sage in addition to the big three (Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent). If you already use Sage, this matters. Starling's built-in tools are good for simple businesses, but if you want a dedicated third-party accounting package, Tide's integration range is wider.

**More detailed expense categorisation.** Tide's auto-categorisation is more granular, which helps at [Self Assessment](/guides/self-assessment/) time. The app learns your spending patterns and gets better over time.

**Multi-company support.** If you run more than one business (or plan to), Tide lets you manage multiple accounts from one login. Starling treats each business as a separate app profile.

## Our recommendation

**For most sole traders: Starling.** It's the better bank, the app is excellent, and the combination of unlimited free transfers, free cash deposits, phone support, and built-in accounting essentials makes it the strongest free offering. You may not even need separate accounting software for a simple business.

**Choose Tide if:** you already use Sage, need more granular expense categorisation, or prefer Tide's approach to managing multiple businesses. Be aware that the free plan's 5-transfer limit means you'll likely need the £12.49/month plan fairly quickly if you're actively trading.

**Or consider Mettle instead of both** if you want free accounting software bundled in. ## Can I switch later?

Yes, easily. Neither account has exit fees or minimum terms. Opening a new account takes 10 minutes. The only admin is updating your payment details with clients and redirecting any standing orders or Direct Debits. Most people complete the switch in a day.

The main thing: don't let the decision delay you. Both accounts are free, both are good, and having any business bank account is better than running everything through your personal one. Pick one, [connect it to your accounting software](/best/accounting-software-sole-trader/), and start trading.
